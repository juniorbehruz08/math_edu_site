Practice with Signs

What does working with signs mean?

Working with signs means deciding whether a result is positive, negative, or zero when numbers are added, subtracted, multiplied, divided, or raised to a power. A sign is not just a small symbol; it changes the position of a number on the number line and can completely change the value of an expression.

In this lesson, we separate the sign of a number from its absolute value. The absolute value is the distance from zero, so it is never negative.

\(-7,\ -2,\ 0,\ 4,\ 9\)
Key idea: the sign tells the direction from zero, while the absolute value tells the distance from zero.
\(|-7| = 7,\quad |7| = 7\)

Positive, negative, and zero

Positive numbers are greater than zero, negative numbers are less than zero, and zero is neither positive nor negative. On the number line, negative numbers are placed to the left of zero and positive numbers to the right.

Type Meaning Examples
Positive numbers Numbers greater than zero. \(3,\ 8,\ 15,\ 0.5\)
Negative numbers Numbers less than zero. \(-1,\ -6,\ -12,\ -0.75\)
Zero The boundary between positive and negative numbers. \(0\)
Important: \(0\) has no sign. It is not positive and it is not negative.

Adding signed numbers

When adding signed numbers, first check whether the signs are the same or different. Think of positive numbers as moving to the right and negative numbers as moving to the left.

Case 1: Same signs

Add the absolute values and keep the common sign.

\(5 + 3 = 8\)
\(-5 + (-3) = -8\)
Case 2: Different signs

Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value, then keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value.

\(-7 + 4 = -3\)
\(9 + (-2) = 7\)
Example A Two negative numbers

Both numbers are negative, so the result stays negative.

\(-12 + (-8) = -20\)
Example B The negative number has larger absolute value

Since \(|-15| > |6|\), the answer is negative.

\(-15 + 6 = -9\)
Example C The positive number has larger absolute value

Since \(|20| > |-7|\), the answer is positive.

\(20 + (-7) = 13\)
Example D Opposite numbers cancel out

Numbers with the same absolute value and opposite signs add to zero.

\(-11 + 11 = 0\)

Subtracting signed numbers

Subtraction is best understood as adding the opposite number. Keep the first number, change subtraction to addition, and change the sign of the second number.

\(a - b = a + (-b)\)
Keep the first number unchanged.
Change the subtraction sign to addition.
Replace the second number by its opposite.
Subtracting a positive number

Subtracting \(7\) means adding \(-7\).

\(4 - 7 = 4 + (-7) = -3\)
Subtracting a negative number

Subtracting \(-7\) means adding \(7\).

\(4 - (-7) = 4 + 7 = 11\)
Negative minus positive

The movement goes farther to the left.

\(-5 - 9 = -5 + (-9) = -14\)
Negative minus negative

Change subtraction into addition of the opposite.

\(-6 - (-10) = -6 + 10 = 4\)
Common mistake: \(4 - (-7)\) is not \(-3\). Two signs together, “minus negative,” become plus.

Multiplication and division with signs

For multiplication and division, the sign depends on the number of negative factors. An even number of negative signs gives a positive result. An odd number of negative signs gives a negative result.

Rule Example Result
Positive × positive \(4 \times 3\) \(12\)
Positive × negative \(4 \times (-3)\) \(-12\)
Negative × positive \((-4) \times 3\) \(-12\)
Negative × negative \((-4) \times (-3)\) \(12\)
More than two factors

There are three negative signs, so the result is negative.

\((-2)(-3)(-4) = -24\)
Division uses the same sign rules

A negative divided by a negative gives a positive result.

\(\dfrac{-18}{-3} = 6\)
Useful method: first calculate the absolute values, then decide the sign at the end.

Signs with exponents

Parentheses are extremely important with powers. If the negative number is inside parentheses, the whole negative number is the base. If there are no parentheses, the exponent applies first and the negative sign stays outside.

With parentheses

The base is \(-4\), so the sign is included in the power.

\((-4)^2 = 16\)
Without parentheses

Only \(4\) is squared, then the negative sign is applied.

\(-4^2 = -(4^2) = -16\)
Even powers

A negative base raised to an even power becomes positive.

\((-3)^4 = 81\)
Odd powers

A negative base raised to an odd power remains negative.

\((-3)^3 = -27\)
Common mistake: \(-5^2\) and \((-5)^2\) are not the same. The first equals \(-25\), while the second equals \(25\).

Signs in fractions

A negative sign in a fraction may be placed in the numerator, in the denominator, or in front of the fraction. These three forms are equivalent if there is exactly one negative sign.

\(\dfrac{-a}{b} = \dfrac{a}{-b} = -\dfrac{a}{b}\)
One negative sign

One negative sign makes the whole fraction negative.

\(\dfrac{-6}{3} = -2\)
Two negative signs

Two negative signs cancel each other.

\(\dfrac{-6}{-3} = 2\)
Do not leave two negative signs in a simplified fraction. For example, \(\dfrac{-2}{-5}\) should be written as \(\dfrac{2}{5}\).

Order of operations with signs

When an expression contains signs and several operations, follow the standard order: parentheses, exponents, multiplication or division, and then addition or subtraction.

Start with: \(2 - 3(-2)\)
Multiply first: \(3(-2) = -6\)
Now subtract a negative: \(2 - (-6) = 8\)

\(8\)

Another example

Start with: \(-4^2 + 3(-2)\)
Apply the exponent first: \(-4^2 = -16\)
Multiply: \(3(-2) = -6\)
Add the results: \(-16 + (-6) = -22\)

\(-22\)

Quick summary

  • Same signs in addition: add absolute values and keep the common sign.
  • Different signs in addition: subtract absolute values and keep the sign of the larger absolute value.
  • Subtraction means adding the opposite.
  • For multiplication and division, an even number of negative signs gives a positive result.
  • For powers, parentheses decide whether the negative sign is part of the base.
  • Zero is neither positive nor negative.
