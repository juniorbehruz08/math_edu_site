Trigonometric Formulas — Modular Guide

This page organizes the most important trigonometric formulas into modules. Instead of one very long page, use the tabs below to study one formula group at a time.

The formulas here include fundamental identities, angle addition and subtraction, double-angle formulas, half-angle formulas, product-to-sum, sum-to-product, power-reduction, and related formulas derived from the main identities.

Main idea: Most trigonometric formulas are not random. They come from a small number of core identities.

1How These Formula Groups Connect

Trigonometric formulas are useful because they allow us to rewrite complicated expressions in simpler or more convenient forms. For example, a product can be changed into a sum, a double angle can be rewritten using one angle, and a half angle can be found from a known angle.

Formula group Purpose Typical use
Fundamental identities Connect sine, cosine, tangent, and cotangent. Simplifying expressions.
Angle addition and subtraction Find trigonometric values of combined angles. Finding \(\sin(75^\circ)\) from \(45^\circ+30^\circ\).
Double-angle formulas Rewrite trig functions of \(2x\). Changing \(\cos(2x)\) into expressions with \(x\).
Half-angle formulas Find trig functions of half an angle. Finding values like \(\sin(15^\circ)\).
Product-to-sum and sum-to-product Convert products into sums or sums into products. Useful in simplification and advanced problem solving.

2Fundamental Identities

These are the base identities. Many later formulas are built from them.

Pythagorean identity
\(\sin^2x+\cos^2x=1\)
Tangent identity
\(\tan x=\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\)
Cotangent identity
\(\cot x=\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}\)
Tangent and cotangent
\(\tan x\cdot\cot x=1\)

Simple Derived Forms

From the main identity \(\sin^2x+\cos^2x=1\), we can isolate either sine squared or cosine squared.

\(\sin^2x=1-\cos^2x\)
\(\cos^2x=1-\sin^2x\)

We can also write the related identities without using extra names like secant or cosecant.

\(1+\tan^2x=\frac{1}{\cos^2x}\)
\(1+\cot^2x=\frac{1}{\sin^2x}\)
Important: Instead of using \(\sec x\) or \(\csc x\), this page writes them as \(\frac{1}{\cos x}\) and \(\frac{1}{\sin x}\).

Examples for the Fundamental Identities

Formula Example
\(\sin^2x+\cos^2x=1\) \(\sin^230^\circ+\cos^230^\circ=\left(\frac12\right)^2+\left(\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\right)^2=1\)
\(\tan x=\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\) \(\tan45^\circ=\frac{\sin45^\circ}{\cos45^\circ}=\frac{\frac{\sqrt2}{2}}{\frac{\sqrt2}{2}}=1\)
\(\cot x=\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}\) \(\cot45^\circ=\frac{\cos45^\circ}{\sin45^\circ}=1\)
\(\tan x\cdot\cot x=1\) \(\tan30^\circ\cdot\cot30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt3}{3}\cdot\sqrt3=1\)
\(\sin^2x=1-\cos^2x\) \(\sin^260^\circ=1-\cos^260^\circ=1-\left(\frac12\right)^2=\frac34\)
\(\cos^2x=1-\sin^2x\) \(\cos^260^\circ=1-\sin^260^\circ=1-\frac34=\frac14\)
\(1+\tan^2x=\frac{1}{\cos^2x}\) \(1+\tan^245^\circ=1+1=2=\frac{1}{\cos^245^\circ}\)
\(1+\cot^2x=\frac{1}{\sin^2x}\) \(1+\cot^245^\circ=1+1=2=\frac{1}{\sin^245^\circ}\)

3Angle Addition and Subtraction Formulas

These formulas help us find trigonometric values of combined angles such as \(x+y\) or \(x-y\).

Formula Meaning
\(\sin(x+y)=\sin x\cos y+\cos x\sin y\) Sine of a sum uses sine-cosine plus cosine-sine.
\(\sin(x-y)=\sin x\cos y-\cos x\sin y\) Sine of a difference uses the same pattern with minus.
\(\cos(x+y)=\cos x\cos y-\sin x\sin y\) Cosine of a sum uses cosine-cosine minus sine-sine.
\(\cos(x-y)=\cos x\cos y+\sin x\sin y\) Cosine of a difference uses cosine-cosine plus sine-sine.
\(\tan(x+y)=\frac{\tan x+\tan y}{1-\tan x\tan y}\) Tangent of a sum has a denominator involving \(1-\tan x\tan y\).
\(\tan(x-y)=\frac{\tan x-\tan y}{1+\tan x\tan y}\) Tangent of a difference has a denominator involving \(1+\tan x\tan y\).
\(\cot(x+y)=\frac{\cot x\cot y-1}{\cot x+\cot y}\) Cotangent of a sum can be written using cotangent values of the two angles.
\(\cot(x-y)=\frac{\cot x\cot y+1}{\cot y-\cot x}\) Cotangent of a difference has a numerator with \(+1\) and a denominator \(\cot y-\cot x\).
Memory warning: Sine keeps the same sign as the angle operation. Cosine changes the sign.

Examples for Angle Addition and Subtraction

Formula Example
\(\sin(x+y)=\sin x\cos y+\cos x\sin y\) \(\sin75^\circ=\sin45^\circ\cos30^\circ+\cos45^\circ\sin30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt6+\sqrt2}{4}\)
\(\sin(x-y)=\sin x\cos y-\cos x\sin y\) \(\sin15^\circ=\sin45^\circ\cos30^\circ-\cos45^\circ\sin30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt6-\sqrt2}{4}\)
\(\cos(x+y)=\cos x\cos y-\sin x\sin y\) \(\cos75^\circ=\cos45^\circ\cos30^\circ-\sin45^\circ\sin30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt6-\sqrt2}{4}\)
\(\cos(x-y)=\cos x\cos y+\sin x\sin y\) \(\cos15^\circ=\cos45^\circ\cos30^\circ+\sin45^\circ\sin30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt6+\sqrt2}{4}\)
\(\tan(x+y)=\frac{\tan x+\tan y}{1-\tan x\tan y}\) \(\tan75^\circ=\frac{1+\frac{\sqrt3}{3}}{1-1\cdot\frac{\sqrt3}{3}}=2+\sqrt3\)
\(\tan(x-y)=\frac{\tan x-\tan y}{1+\tan x\tan y}\) \(\tan15^\circ=\frac{1-\frac{\sqrt3}{3}}{1+1\cdot\frac{\sqrt3}{3}}=2-\sqrt3\)
\(\cot(x+y)=\frac{\cot x\cot y-1}{\cot x+\cot y}\) \(\cot75^\circ=\frac{1\cdot\sqrt3-1}{1+\sqrt3}=2-\sqrt3\)
\(\cot(x-y)=\frac{\cot x\cot y+1}{\cot y-\cot x}\) \(\cot15^\circ=\frac{1\cdot\sqrt3+1}{\sqrt3-1}=2+\sqrt3\)

4Double-Angle Formulas

Double-angle formulas come from angle addition formulas by setting both angles equal.

Sine double angle
\(\sin(2x)=2\sin x\cos x\)
Cosine double angle
\(\cos(2x)=\cos^2x-\sin^2x\)
Cosine version 2
\(\cos(2x)=2\cos^2x-1\)
Cosine version 3
\(\cos(2x)=1-2\sin^2x\)
Tangent double angle
\(\tan(2x)=\frac{2\tan x}{1-\tan^2x}\)
Cotangent double angle
\(\cot(2x)=\frac{\cot^2x-1}{2\cot x}\)

How one formula derives from another

Starting from \(\cos(2x)=\cos^2x-\sin^2x\), use \(\sin^2x+\cos^2x=1\).

Replace \(\sin^2x\) with \(1-\cos^2x\).
Then \(\cos(2x)=\cos^2x-(1-\cos^2x)\).
So \(\cos(2x)=2\cos^2x-1\).
Main point: The three cosine double-angle formulas are different forms of the same identity.

Examples for Double-Angle Formulas

Formula Example
\(\sin(2x)=2\sin x\cos x\) \(\sin60^\circ=2\sin30^\circ\cos30^\circ=2\cdot\frac12\cdot\frac{\sqrt3}{2}=\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\)
\(\cos(2x)=\cos^2x-\sin^2x\) \(\cos60^\circ=\cos^230^\circ-\sin^230^\circ=\frac34-\frac14=\frac12\)
\(\cos(2x)=2\cos^2x-1\) \(\cos60^\circ=2\cos^230^\circ-1=2\cdot\frac34-1=\frac12\)
\(\cos(2x)=1-2\sin^2x\) \(\cos60^\circ=1-2\sin^230^\circ=1-2\cdot\frac14=\frac12\)
\(\tan(2x)=\frac{2\tan x}{1-\tan^2x}\) \(\tan60^\circ=\frac{2\tan30^\circ}{1-\tan^230^\circ}=\sqrt3\)
\(\cot(2x)=\frac{\cot^2x-1}{2\cot x}\) \(\cot60^\circ=\frac{\cot^230^\circ-1}{2\cot30^\circ}=\frac{\sqrt3}{3}\)

5Half-Angle Formulas

Half-angle formulas help us find trigonometric values of \(\frac{x}{2}\). They come from the cosine double-angle identities.

Function Formula Sign note
\(\sin\frac{x}{2}\) \(\sin\frac{x}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos x}{2}}\) Choose the sign based on the quadrant of \(\frac{x}{2}\).
\(\cos\frac{x}{2}\) \(\cos\frac{x}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{2}}\) Choose the sign based on the quadrant of \(\frac{x}{2}\).
\(\tan\frac{x}{2}\) \(\tan\frac{x}{2}=\frac{\sin x}{1+\cos x}\) Useful when \(1+\cos x\ne0\).
\(\tan\frac{x}{2}\) \(\tan\frac{x}{2}=\frac{1-\cos x}{\sin x}\) Useful when \(\sin x\ne0\).
\(\cot\frac{x}{2}\) \(\cot\frac{x}{2}=\frac{\sin x}{1-\cos x}\) Useful when \(1-\cos x\ne0\).
\(\cot\frac{x}{2}\) \(\cot\frac{x}{2}=\frac{1+\cos x}{\sin x}\) Useful when \(\sin x\ne0\).

Where half-angle formulas come from

From the double-angle identity \(\cos(2u)=1-2\sin^2u\), solve for \(\sin^2u\). Then let \(u=\frac{x}{2}\).

\(\sin^2\frac{x}{2}=\frac{1-\cos x}{2}\)

Taking the square root gives the half-angle formula for sine.

Important: The \(\pm\) sign is not optional decoration. The correct sign depends on where the half-angle lies.

Examples for Half-Angle Formulas

Formula Example
\(\sin\frac{x}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos x}{2}}\) \(\sin15^\circ=\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos30^\circ}{2}}=\sqrt{\frac{1-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}}{2}}\)
\(\cos\frac{x}{2}=\pm\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{2}}\) \(\cos15^\circ=\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos30^\circ}{2}}=\sqrt{\frac{1+\frac{\sqrt3}{2}}{2}}\)
\(\tan\frac{x}{2}=\frac{\sin x}{1+\cos x}\) \(\tan15^\circ=\frac{\sin30^\circ}{1+\cos30^\circ}=\frac{\frac12}{1+\frac{\sqrt3}{2}}=2-\sqrt3\)
\(\tan\frac{x}{2}=\frac{1-\cos x}{\sin x}\) \(\tan15^\circ=\frac{1-\cos30^\circ}{\sin30^\circ}=\frac{1-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}}{\frac12}=2-\sqrt3\)
\(\cot\frac{x}{2}=\frac{\sin x}{1-\cos x}\) \(\cot15^\circ=\frac{\sin30^\circ}{1-\cos30^\circ}=\frac{\frac12}{1-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}}=2+\sqrt3\)
\(\cot\frac{x}{2}=\frac{1+\cos x}{\sin x}\) \(\cot15^\circ=\frac{1+\cos30^\circ}{\sin30^\circ}=\frac{1+\frac{\sqrt3}{2}}{\frac12}=2+\sqrt3\)

6Product-to-Sum and Sum-to-Product Formulas

These formulas are useful when a problem contains products of trigonometric functions or sums of trigonometric functions.

Product-to-Sum

Original expression Rewritten form
\(\sin x\sin y\) \(\frac{1}{2}\left[\cos(x-y)-\cos(x+y)\right]\)
\(\cos x\cos y\) \(\frac{1}{2}\left[\cos(x-y)+\cos(x+y)\right]\)
\(\sin x\cos y\) \(\frac{1}{2}\left[\sin(x+y)+\sin(x-y)\right]\)
\(\cos x\sin y\) \(\frac{1}{2}\left[\sin(x+y)-\sin(x-y)\right]\)

Sum-to-Product

Original expression Rewritten form
\(\sin x+\sin y\) \(2\sin\frac{x+y}{2}\cos\frac{x-y}{2}\)
\(\sin x-\sin y\) \(2\cos\frac{x+y}{2}\sin\frac{x-y}{2}\)
\(\cos x+\cos y\) \(2\cos\frac{x+y}{2}\cos\frac{x-y}{2}\)
\(\cos x-\cos y\) \(-2\sin\frac{x+y}{2}\sin\frac{x-y}{2}\)
Connection: Product-to-sum and sum-to-product formulas are closely connected to angle addition and subtraction formulas.

Examples for Product-to-Sum and Sum-to-Product

Formula Example
\(\sin x\sin y=\frac{1}{2}\left[\cos(x-y)-\cos(x+y)\right]\) \(\sin60^\circ\sin30^\circ=\frac12[\cos30^\circ-\cos90^\circ]=\frac{\sqrt3}{4}\)
\(\cos x\cos y=\frac{1}{2}\left[\cos(x-y)+\cos(x+y)\right]\) \(\cos60^\circ\cos30^\circ=\frac12[\cos30^\circ+\cos90^\circ]=\frac{\sqrt3}{4}\)
\(\sin x\cos y=\frac{1}{2}\left[\sin(x+y)+\sin(x-y)\right]\) \(\sin60^\circ\cos30^\circ=\frac12[\sin90^\circ+\sin30^\circ]=\frac34\)
\(\cos x\sin y=\frac{1}{2}\left[\sin(x+y)-\sin(x-y)\right]\) \(\cos60^\circ\sin30^\circ=\frac12[\sin90^\circ-\sin30^\circ]=\frac14\)
\(\sin x+\sin y=2\sin\frac{x+y}{2}\cos\frac{x-y}{2}\) \(\sin75^\circ+\sin15^\circ=2\sin45^\circ\cos30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt6}{2}\)
\(\sin x-\sin y=2\cos\frac{x+y}{2}\sin\frac{x-y}{2}\) \(\sin75^\circ-\sin15^\circ=2\cos45^\circ\sin30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\)
\(\cos x+\cos y=2\cos\frac{x+y}{2}\cos\frac{x-y}{2}\) \(\cos75^\circ+\cos15^\circ=2\cos45^\circ\cos30^\circ=\frac{\sqrt6}{2}\)
\(\cos x-\cos y=-2\sin\frac{x+y}{2}\sin\frac{x-y}{2}\) \(\cos75^\circ-\cos15^\circ=-2\sin45^\circ\sin30^\circ=-\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\)

7Power-Reduction and Related Formulas

Power-reduction formulas rewrite powers such as \(\sin^2x\) and \(\cos^2x\) using double angles. They come from the cosine double-angle formulas.

Sine squared
\(\sin^2x=\frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}\)
Cosine squared
\(\cos^2x=\frac{1+\cos(2x)}{2}\)
Tangent squared
\(\tan^2x=\frac{1}{\cos^2x}-1\)
Cotangent squared
\(\cot^2x=\frac{1}{\sin^2x}-1\)

Derived from double-angle formulas

From \(\cos(2x)=1-2\sin^2x\), solve for \(\sin^2x\).

\(2\sin^2x=1-\cos(2x)\)
\(\sin^2x=\frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}\)

From \(\cos(2x)=2\cos^2x-1\), solve for \(\cos^2x\).

\(\cos^2x=\frac{1+\cos(2x)}{2}\)
Use case: Power-reduction formulas are helpful when expressions contain squared trigonometric functions.

Examples for Power-Reduction and Related Formulas

Formula Example
\(\sin^2x=\frac{1-\cos(2x)}{2}\) \(\sin^230^\circ=\frac{1-\cos60^\circ}{2}=\frac{1-\frac12}{2}=\frac14\)
\(\cos^2x=\frac{1+\cos(2x)}{2}\) \(\cos^230^\circ=\frac{1+\cos60^\circ}{2}=\frac{1+\frac12}{2}=\frac34\)
\(\tan^2x=\frac{1}{\cos^2x}-1\) \(\tan^245^\circ=\frac{1}{\cos^245^\circ}-1=2-1=1\)
\(\cot^2x=\frac{1}{\sin^2x}-1\) \(\cot^245^\circ=\frac{1}{\sin^245^\circ}-1=2-1=1\)

This section gives a compact checklist of the most important formulas from the modules.
