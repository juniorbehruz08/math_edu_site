Main Types of Numbers

Naturals numbers are that we use to count

  • For example: 1,2,3,4, ....
  • 0 is not a natural number.
  • Since we do not use negative numbers to count, negative numbers are not natural.

Whole numbers include all positive numbers and 0

  • For example: 0,1,2,3,4, ...
  • Negative numbers are not included
  • The difference between whole numbers and natural numbers is that whole numbers include 0.

Integers are all whole numbers, including positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero.

  • For example: -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ...
  • -7 is a negative integer, while 7 is a positive integer
  • 0 is neutral

Numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers, where the denominator is not zero.

  • For example, 5 is a rational number since it can be written as 5/1 or 10/2.
  • Some numbers cannot be a rational numbers: π, √2, √3

Numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers

  • For example: √2, √3, π
  • Their decimal expansions are non-terminating and non-repeating
  • Irrational numbers are used when quantities cannot be measured exactly as a simple fraction.

All rational and irrational numbers.

  • For example: 3/4, 5, -3, π
  • They can be represented on the number line and are used to measure continuous quantities.

Numbers that are multiples of i, where i² = -1

  • For example: i, 3i, -5i
  • i⁴ = 1
  • i = √(-1)

A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.

  • For example: 2,3,5,7,11
  • 1 is not considered prime
  • Smallest prime number is 2

A composite number is a natural number greater than 1 that has at least one positive divisor other than 1 and itself.

  • For example: 6, 8, 9, 12
  • They can be expressed as a product of prime numbers
  • Numbers 0 and 1 are neither prime nor composite
