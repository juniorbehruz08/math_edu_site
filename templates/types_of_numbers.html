What are number sets?

Number sets are groups used to classify numbers according to their properties. The same number can belong to several sets at the same time. For example, \(5\) is a natural number, a whole number, an integer, a rational number, and a real number.

Main number sets

Set Definition Examples
Natural numbers Numbers used for counting. In this lesson, natural numbers start from \(1\): \(1,2,3,\ldots\). \(1,2,3,4,\ldots\)
Whole numbers The non-negative integers: \(0\) together with the natural numbers. \(0,1,2,3,\ldots\)
Integers All whole numbers and their negatives, including \(0\). Integers do not have fractional or decimal parts. \(\ldots,-2,-1,0,1,2,\ldots\)
Rational numbers Numbers that can be written in the form \(\dfrac{a}{b}\), where \(a\) and \(b\) are integers and \(b \ne 0\). Terminating and repeating decimals are rational. \(\dfrac{3}{4}, -2, 0.5, 7\)
Irrational numbers Real numbers that cannot be written as a ratio of two integers. Their decimal expansions are non-terminating and non-repeating. \(\sqrt{2}, \pi, \sqrt{3}\)
Real numbers All numbers that can be represented on the number line. The real numbers consist of rational and irrational numbers. \(-4, 0, \dfrac{2}{3}, \sqrt{5}\)

Numbers on the number line

Every real number has a position on the number line. Negative numbers lie to the left of \(0\), while positive numbers lie to the right of \(0\).

-3 0 2 -5 -2.5 0 2.5 5
Key idea: \(\mathbb{N} \subset \mathbb{W} \subset \mathbb{Z} \subset \mathbb{Q} \subset \mathbb{R}\). Every natural number is real, but not every real number is natural. For example, \(\sqrt{2}\) is real but not rational or natural.

Prime and composite numbers

Prime numbers

A prime number is a natural number greater than \(1\) that has exactly two positive divisors: \(1\) and itself.

\(2,\ 3,\ 5,\ 7,\ 11,\ 13\)

Composite numbers

A composite number is a natural number greater than \(1\) that has more than two positive divisors.

\(4,\ 6,\ 8,\ 9,\ 10,\ 12\)
The number \(1\) is neither prime nor composite because it has only one positive divisor.
