Trigonometry — Sine, Cosine, Tangent, and Cotangent

Trigonometry studies relationships between angles and numbers. The four basic trigonometric functions in this lesson are sine, cosine, tangent, and cotangent.

They can be understood from a right triangle and from the unit circle. In this lesson, we focus on general meaning, domains, ranges, important values, and round unit-circle graphs.

Main idea: An angle can be connected to a point on a circle, and the coordinates of that point help define trigonometric values.

1The Unit Circle Idea

A unit circle is a circle with radius \(1\), centered at the origin. Every angle creates a point on this circle.

\((x,y)=(\cos\theta,\sin\theta)\)

This means cosine is connected to the horizontal coordinate, and sine is connected to the vertical coordinate.

Cosine

Cosine gives the \(x\)-coordinate of the point on the unit circle.

Sine

Sine gives the \(y\)-coordinate of the point on the unit circle.

Tangent

Tangent compares sine to cosine.

Cotangent

Cotangent compares cosine to sine.

2General Meaning of the Four Functions

Function Unit-circle meaning Right-triangle meaning
\(\sin\theta\) The \(y\)-coordinate on the unit circle. Opposite side divided by hypotenuse.
\(\cos\theta\) The \(x\)-coordinate on the unit circle. Adjacent side divided by hypotenuse.
\(\tan\theta\) The ratio of sine to cosine. Opposite side divided by adjacent side.
\(\cot\theta\) The ratio of cosine to sine. Adjacent side divided by opposite side.

3Domain and Range

The domain tells which angle inputs are allowed. The range tells which output values are possible.

Function Domain Range Important note
\(\sin\theta\) All real numbers \([-1,1]\) Always defined.
\(\cos\theta\) All real numbers \([-1,1]\) Always defined.
\(\tan\theta\) \(\theta\ne90^\circ+180^\circ k,\ k\in\mathbb Z\) All real numbers Undefined when cosine is \(0\).
\(\cot\theta\) \(\theta\ne180^\circ k,\ k\in\mathbb Z\) All real numbers Undefined when sine is \(0\).

Important: Tangent is undefined when cosine is \(0\). Cotangent is undefined when sine is \(0\).

5Signs in the Four Quadrants

The sign of each trigonometric value depends on the quadrant where the angle lands.

Quadrant Angle range \(\sin\theta\) \(\cos\theta\) \(\tan\theta\) \(\cot\theta\)
I \(0^\circ<\theta<90^\circ\) + + + +
II \(90^\circ<\theta<180^\circ\) + - - -
III \(180^\circ<\theta<270^\circ\) - - + +
IV \(270^\circ<\theta<360^\circ\) - + - -

6Round Graph for Sine

Move the angle to see the sine value on a round unit-circle graph. The sine value is shown by the vertical coordinate.

Sine value
\(\sin(30^\circ)=0.500\)

Positive sine values are above the center. Negative sine values are below the center.

7Round Graph for Cosine

Move the angle to see the cosine value on a round unit-circle graph. The cosine value is shown by the horizontal coordinate.

Cosine value
\(\cos(30^\circ)=0.866\)

Positive cosine values are to the right of the center. Negative cosine values are to the left.

8Round Graph for Tangent

Move the angle to see the tangent value. Tangent is undefined when the point is directly above or below the center.

Tangent value
\(\tan(30^\circ)=0.577\)

Tangent becomes undefined at \(90^\circ\) and \(270^\circ\), where cosine is \(0\).

9Round Graph for Cotangent

Move the angle to see the cotangent value. Cotangent is undefined when the point is on the left or right side of the circle.

Cotangent value
\(\cot(30^\circ)=1.732\)

Cotangent becomes undefined at \(0^\circ\), \(180^\circ\), and \(360^\circ\), where sine is \(0\).

10Repeating Pattern

Trigonometric values repeat after a full turn. A full turn is \(360^\circ\), or \(2\pi\) radians.

Sine and cosine

Their values repeat every \(360^\circ\).

Tangent and cotangent

Their values repeat every \(180^\circ\).

Simple meaning: Once you understand one full circle, you can understand many larger angles by looking at where they land on the circle.

11Final Summary

  1. Sine is connected to the vertical coordinate on the unit circle.
  2. Cosine is connected to the horizontal coordinate on the unit circle.
  3. Tangent compares sine to cosine.
  4. Cotangent compares cosine to sine.
  5. Sine and cosine have range \([-1,1]\).
  6. Tangent and cotangent can have any real output value.
  7. Tangent is undefined where cosine is \(0\).
  8. Cotangent is undefined where sine is \(0\).
  9. The most important angles are \(0^\circ\), \(30^\circ\), \(45^\circ\), \(60^\circ\), and \(90^\circ\), plus their related angles around the circle.
