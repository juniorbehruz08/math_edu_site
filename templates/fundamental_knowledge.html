Fundamentals of Mathematics

Mathematics is the study of quantity, structure, space, and change. It provides a logical and abstract framework for understanding and modeling the natural world. Below is an overview of the fundamental branches of mathematics and their key concepts.

1. Main Branches of Mathematics

Arithmetic is the study of numbers and basic operations.

  • Natural numbers: 1, 2, 3, …
  • Integers: … -2, -1, 0, 1, 2 …
  • Rational numbers: a/b where b ≠ 0
  • Irrational numbers: √2, π, e …
  • Complex numbers: a + bi, where i² = -1

Algebra deals with symbols and rules for manipulating them.

  • Equations and inequalities
  • Functions: linear, quadratic, exponential
  • Matrices and determinants

Geometry studies shapes, sizes, and properties of space.

  • Euclidean: Triangles, circles
  • Analytic: Coordinates and equations
  • Affine and projective geometry

Trigonometry studies relations between angles and sides.

  • Sine, Cosine, Tangent, Cotangent
  • Trigonometric identities
  • Limits and continuity
  • Differentiation
  • Integration
  • Series and convergence
  • Combinatorics
  • Distributions: Normal, Poisson, Binomial
  • Statistical analysis & regression
  • Logic and set theory
  • Graph theory
  • Algorithms and computation

2. Mathematical Logic and Proofs

Mathematics is built on logical reasoning and proof techniques.

  • Axioms and Theorems
  • Proof methods: direct, contradiction, induction

Example: Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c²

3. Applications of Mathematics

  • Physics – mechanics, quantum theory
  • Engineering – electronics, automation
  • Economics – financial modeling
  • Computer Science – algorithms, AI
  • Astronomy – orbital mechanics

Conclusion

Mathematics is the foundation of science and technology, providing essential tools for problem-solving and discovery.
